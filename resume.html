Summary

I have an interest in mathematical formalization and its implementation on computers.

My motivation of research has been formalization of reasoning.
That is, my aim is to propose a mathematical definition of reasoning and implement it.
Thinking so, I majored computer science and learned especially mathematical logic and programming in the university.

In that process, I got interest in the design and implementation of programming languages.
Pursuit of direct representation of computer programs suited me very much.
Then, I got an idea of the mechanism to strengthen the expressive power of pattern-matching expressions and implemented it in my new programming language Egison.
For detail on Egison, please see the website and my latest presentation.

Recently, I have interest in importing ideas of mathematics behind machine learning technology into programming languages.
There are still several notations and modularization that are possible on papers but impossible on computer programs.
My research purpose is to fill these gaps and accelerate research of science.

Activities

The Egison Programming Language (Ongoing)

https://www.egison.org

I have designed and implemented programming language Egison. Egison is a programming language whose feature is non-linear pattern-matching with backtracking. We can directly represent pattern-matching against lists, multisets, sets, trees, graphs and any kind of data types.

Egison proposes a new paradigm pattern-matching-oriented. The combination of all of the following features enables intuitive powerful pattern-matching.

  • Modularization of the way of pattern-matching
  • Pattern-matching with backtracking
  • Non-linear patterns with lexical scoping

Egison is implemented in Haskell and distributed as a hackage package. Egison is getting popular and attracting a lot of people.

The source code is available on GitHub.

My latest presentation on Egison at Rakuten Technology Conference 2015 is here.
